Why Assam Is the Gateway to Northeast India

Assam has always been the entry point to India's most geographically diverse region — the Northeast. With Guwahati serving as the commercial and transport hub, every journey to Meghalaya, Arunachal Pradesh, Nagaland, Mizoram, or Manipur begins here.

From the Brahmaputra riverfront to the Kamakhya Temple atop Nilachal Hill, Assam itself is a destination that demands exploration. The state's biodiversity, culture, cuisine, and silk heritage make it unlike anywhere else in India.

As someone who has been documenting Assam for years as one of the best bloggers in the region, I can tell you — Assam is not a pitstop. It's a destination.

02

Best Time to Visit Assam — A Month-by-Month Guide

Assam's travel calendar is shaped by the Brahmaputra, the monsoon, and its famous wildlife cycles. October to April is the prime travel window — Kaziranga reopens, the weather is crisp, and the landscape is at its most vibrant after the rains.

The Bihu festivals in April and October bring Assam's culture to life in ways no tourist experience can replicate. February brings the Elephant Festival near Kaziranga. March sees the Orchid Festival in Jorhat.

If you're asking a local — and I am one — the best months are November and February. The fog lifts, rhinos are visible, and the Northeast shimmers.

How to Become a Travel Content Creator in Northeast India

Being a content creator in Northeast India comes with unique challenges and extraordinary rewards. The region is one of India's most under-documented — which means if you're creating here, you're often a pioneer.

My journey started with Instagram, sharing mini vlogs and travel photos from around Assam. Over time, I built a community by focusing on authenticity over aesthetics — showing the real Northeast, not just the highlight reel.

For aspiring Assamese content creators: start local, be consistent, and always prioritise your community. The Northeast has stories the world hasn't heard yet. You could be the one to tell them.
